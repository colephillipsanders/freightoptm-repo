Data & Methods

The project starts from the full FAF5.7.1 freight flows table, filters to a 2024-style subset, and engineers mode-specific cost and CO₂ estimates at the shipment level.

Data sources

Primary data: FAF5 freight flows by domestic regions, including tonnage, value, distance, and mode. Additional assumptions are used to derive cost and emissions factors.

  • FAF domestic regions (dms_orig, dms_dest)
  • Mode codes and names (road, rail, intermodal)
  • Commodity (sctg2), trade type, distance bands
  • Tons, ton-miles, distance (miles / km)
  • Mode-specific cost & CO₂ per tonne-km
Pandas preprocessing Chunked CSV reading Feature engineering

Methodology

The pipeline cleans the raw FAF5 file, computes estimated transportation cost and CO₂, and prepares separate feature sets for mode choice and cost/emission prediction.

  • Filtering to a 2024 subset of relevant flows
  • Deriving cost / CO₂ from tonne-km and mode factors
  • Splitting into train/test sets for ML models
  • Using pipelines for preprocessing and scaling

Models & Experiments

The capstone experiments include mode choice classification, cost prediction, and multi-objective optimization that incorporates CO₂ emissions into decision-making.

Mode choice classification

A Random Forest classifier predicts the likely transportation mode (road, rail, intermodal) given origin/destination, distance, commodity, and value.

  • Scikit-learn RandomForestClassifier with preprocessing pipeline
  • Evaluation with accuracy, precision/recall, and confusion matrix
  • Helps understand where rail or intermodal may be viable alternatives to road
Random Forest Classification report Confusion matrix

Cost & CO₂ modeling

Regression models estimate total transportation cost and emissions, and a combined cost function explores trade-offs between dollars and kg CO₂e.

  • Baseline cost model using engineered cost per tonne-km
  • CO₂ as an additional factor rather than a single objective
  • Multi-objective experiments: blended cost + emissions
